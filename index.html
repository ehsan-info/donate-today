Food for a family

The people who are affected by floods are appealing for government intervention in the looming food shortages. The affected communities lost their crops, whole others lost animals that either drowned or got stolen after the owners abandoned them due to the flooding. Sylhet this year experienced the worst flooding in decades which affected 500 000 people, displaced 60 000, while 19 000 were relocated to relocation camps in the affected regions.

Shelter for a family

Eight people were killed – five from one family – while thousands more have had their homes washed away or flooded, while communal latrines are unusable and clean water unavailable. Home gardens which allow households to grow their own food have also been destroyed. The rains are ongoing and prolonged, and the level of damage is expected to increase over the coming days. The people affected by fires in March are now severely impacted by flooding.

Education for a child

Severe weather poses all sorts of challenges for educators and students. It can force schools to shut temporarily and keep kids at home. It can cause infrastructure damage, which has been happening in Sylhet. There, job losses, coastal erosion and floods caused people to move away, leading to diminishing school enrollment and, in some cases, permanent school closures. Those population changes, sometimes called “climate migration,” starve some schools of resources and lead to overcrowding in others.
